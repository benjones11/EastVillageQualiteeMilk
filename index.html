Branding
The "Q" Mark
The embossed "Q" identified the bottle as Qualitee's property. Each dairy marked its own bottles so they could be reclaimed during the deposit/return process.
Material
Cylindrical Base
The cylindrical body of the milk bottle dominated the market. This style of milk bottle was believed to be patented by "cylindrical Lester milk jar" in early 1878.

East Village Green & Qualitee Milk Co.

Founded in 1923, the Qualitee Dairy Products Company grew into one of the region's major retail and wholesale dairies its bottles circulating through thousands of households across the city.

1923
Origins

Founded 1923

The Qualitee Dairy Products Company was established by a founding team of San Diego County dairymen, with Douglas Young as general manager. The company would go on to become a major player in the regional dairy industry, as a hub for milk production, distribution, and retail sales. The company sourced raw milk from local farms, processed the milk at their location and distributed it to retailers throughout the region. While the company has long ceased operations, its legacy remains an important part of San Diego's industrial history. The building is now a four-building condominium complex called "ICON".

Notable suppliers include Louis Melville, a rancher originally from the Lakeside/El Cajon area. He and his family hauled milk in cans to the railroad station by hourse and buggy, shipping to Qualitee Dairy until about 1957.
